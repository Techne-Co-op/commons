Wave-Boundary Authoring Guide

How the ledger writes its own next chapter

Sixteen ledger entries are stubs on purpose: W3.1 through W3.7 and W4.1 through W4.7, plus the seeded scopes of W5.0 and W6.0, carry summary lines instead of briefs because detail invented early is detail invented wrong. This guide is the procedure that turns a stub into a brief at the boundary where the detail becomes real, and it is the last artifact this collection needs from outside itself.

01 · What a Boundary Is

A wave boundary is an ordinary ledger step whose deliverable is milestone files. W3.0 depends on the coarse token W1, meaning every W1 step verified; its success state is that W3.1 through W3.7 exist as schema-complete entries; its verification is ci:milestone-validate, the same gatekeeper as everything else. Nothing about authoring is special except its subject matter, and that is the point: the plan extends through the same PR, review, and validation discipline as the code, so the history of the plan stays part of the audit trail of the build (GOVERNANCE.md, amending the plan).

Authoring is agent-executable under the AGENTS.md contract; adoption is human, through review of the boundary PR. The boundary author proposes structure; it decides nothing. Open decisions stay in docs/decisions/, and a brief that quietly resolves one fails review.

02 · Preconditions and Inputs

Before authoring begins, three things must be true: the boundary step's own dependencies are verified, so the wave being detailed sits on built reality rather than intention; the findings routed to this boundary (§04) have been read; and the system as it actually exists has been inspected, meaning the live migrations, the views that exist, and the matrix and fixture files as they stand after earlier waves amended them.

The reading list for any boundary, in order: the seeded entries themselves; milestones/index.json for the wave's current graph; the C6 schema card entries for every table the wave touches, including silences; the C7 matrix rows for those tables, especially clay cells awaiting adoption; the C4 fixture inventory and the invariants; the decision records that gate or activate anything in the wave; docs/citations.md for the addresses the briefs will cite; and docs/findings.md, the register §04 establishes. The plan document itself is background, not source: where the plan and the built system disagree, the built system wins and the disagreement becomes a finding.

03 · The Procedure

Six moves, in order, all inside one branch named step/W<n>.0-wave-briefs.

Inventory. List the wave's entries with detail: seeded from index.json. Each will end the PR as briefed, split, or retired; none may remain seeded, and none may vanish without a recorded rationale in the PR body.

Gather. Pull the boundary's routed findings from the register and the wave's open decisions. Each routed item must surface in exactly one brief, or convert to a decision record, or be explicitly re-deferred with a new finding number. Silence about a routed finding fails the definition of done.

Decompose. Size each entry to one PR of work: one schema concern, one surface, or one workflow per step. A seeded entry that turns out to contain two migrations and an interface is two or three entries; allocate new ids by appending to the wave (W3.8 onward) rather than renumbering, since ids are immutable once committed.

Edge. Replace the coarse boundary dependency with real parents. After authoring, an entry depends on the boundary step only if it genuinely has no other parent; in-wave fine edges (W3.2 after W3.1) and cross-wave fine edges (W3.4 after W0.10) are both expected. Decision gates move into decision_refs where they bind, as D10 binds W3.6. Later boundaries keep their coarse token: W4.0 still reads depends_on: [W3], because that edge is real at this altitude.

Write. Fill the form per §05. The discipline that matters most: a success state is a falsifiable observation about the system, written against tables and views that will exist, checkable by the named verification.

Extend. This is the move that makes the collection self-extending, and it is mandatory, not courteous. Any new table the wave introduces gets, in the same PR: a C6 schema-card entry at docs/schema-card.md with sources tagged; a C7 matrix row at docs/probe-matrix.json with every role's cell cited, clay where proposed; C4 fixture rows and any new persona at supabase/seed/fixtures.json, with counts declared forced or chosen; and register updates to docs/citations.md for any new address and docs/findings.md for anything surfaced. The collection's artifacts have repo homes precisely so that boundaries grow them by PR instead of waiting for a hand like mine.

05 · Required Sections and Rules

Front matter exactly per milestones/schema.json, with detail flipped from seeded to briefed in the authoring PR and status left at pending; the boundary authors work, it does not start it. The body carries Brief and Plain language as everywhere else, and authored entries add a third section, Verification notes, naming the probes or pgTAP suites the success state implies, with pointers into the matrix rows and fixtures that serve them.

Four rules bind the writing. Falsifiability: a success state describes an observable end state, never activity; "migrations exist and pgTAP proves a credit without a matching debit fails" passes, "implement capital accounts" does not. Named verification: the verification field names a CI job from docs/VERIFICATION.md or a human role from the registry; inventing a job name creates an obligation to spec it in the same PR. Citation: every claim of authority uses the D7 register addresses, and a brief needing an address the register lacks adds it there, flagged draft. Vocabulary: grammar-lint applies to briefs as to everything; the boundary author writes in Subchapter K's tongue or not at all.

07 · Definition of Done

The boundary PR merges when every line below holds, and the PR body states each with its proof, in the order given.

no seeded entries remain in the waveindex.json shows detail: briefed throughout, or the PR records split/retire rationale
no orphan coarse edgesno in-wave entry depends solely on the boundary step without stated cause
routed findings each addressedfindings register updated: resolved-in-brief, converted-to-decision, or re-deferred with new number
collection homes extendedschema card, matrix, fixtures, citations diffs present for every new table or address
success states falsifiablehuman reviewer attests; any "implement X" phrasing fails
validator greenci:milestone-validate on the head commit
nothing decideddocs/decisions diffs contain context only; no Record of decision written by the author
08 · Anti-Patterns

Named so review can point at them. Authoring early: writing W4 briefs while W3 is unbuilt reintroduces the invented detail the seeding avoided; the precondition exists to prevent it. Plan transcription: copying the plan's summary line into success_state without making it falsifiable against built reality; the plan is background. Quiet resolution: a brief whose wording forecloses an open decision or adopts a clay cell; adoption has its own PRs. Scope smuggling: new capability appearing inside an existing entry's brief instead of as a new entry; the ledger is exhaustive or it is nothing. Verification softening: renaming a hard check to a human review because the check is inconvenient; AGENTS.md rule six applies to authors as to builders. Token lingering: leaving depends_on: [W3.0] on entries that have real parents, which flattens the graph the instrument exists to show.

09 · Closing the Series

With this guide the collection is complete in the only sense that matters: C1 governs conduct, C2 holds the ledger, C3 makes verification specifiable, C4 through C9 give the build its references and its test substance, and C10 makes the next references a product of the ledger itself. Wave briefs come from boundary steps; schema cards, matrix rows, fixtures, citations, and findings grow through the Extend move; the map at C0 takes one final status edit and then describes a closed loop. What never enters the loop is deliberate and worth restating at the end: decisions and ratifications, the adoption of every clay cell, chosen count, and open reading, the counsel questions, and the practice phase P1, which is lived rather than shipped. The system can now write its own next chapter; choosing the story remains, as it should, with the people whose cooperative this is.
