Common Information System

The cooperative’s shared knowledge layer — private alpha build workspace
What it is

A cooperative needs to know itself

The Common Information System is RegenHub’s answer to a simple problem: cooperatives make decisions together, but they can only make good decisions if they share an accurate picture of what is happening. The CIS is that picture — a structured record of the co-op’s resources, activities, agreements, and members, kept in one place and accessible to everyone with a legitimate reason to see it.

This is not an app for doing work. It is the layer that remembers what has been done: who the members are, what agreements they have signed, how much patronage each person has earned, what the Hub’s grant has funded, what the co-op owes and is owed. When a member asks “what is my account?” the CIS answers. When a grant funder asks “what events happened?” the CIS answers.

The CIS is built on REA accounting — a framework developed for cooperative and networked organizations. REA stands for Resources, Events, and Agents. These three types cover everything an economic system needs to record.

R
Resources
What the co-op holds or exchanges: money, services, labor, time, digital credits. Every resource has a type, a custodian, and a history.
E
Events
What happens: a member works a shift, a payment arrives, an agreement is signed, patronage is allocated. Events are the source of truth — they do not change after the fact.
A
Agents
Who participates: members, the cooperative itself, external partners, the Hub, grant funders. Each agent holds roles that define what they can see and do.

From these three types the CIS derives everything else: capital accounts, patronage calculations, grant reports, role-based access controls, governance records. None of it depends on a central authority certifying what happened — the events speak for themselves.

Why now

Two facts make the CIS urgent. The cooperative was filed February 6, 2026 and has immediate obligations: member capital accounts, bylaw compliance, records that counsel can rely on. At the same time, RegenHub received an Ethereum Everywhere grant ($20,000, running May through December 2026) to operate the Boulder Ethereum Community Hub — and that grant requires honest reporting on real events. The CIS is how those reports become trustworthy rather than improvised.

What this repository is

This is the build workspace. Everything needed to construct the CIS private alpha lives here: the full specification, a step-by-step build plan with 70 ordered milestones, an operating contract for the agents doing the work, and decision records that unlock the build tree. The build is designed for humans and AI agents working together under an explicit contract — AGENTS.md — that defines what agents may do, what they may not do, and when to stop and ask rather than invent.

Repository

Structure and contracts

The repository is organized so that both humans and agents can navigate without ambiguity. Every directory has a defined role. Every file is either a milestone, a decision, a contract, a specification, or part of the build infrastructure that validates them.
