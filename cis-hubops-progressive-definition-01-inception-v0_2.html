02 · Problem Brief · D-01.1

D-01.1 · AWhat is happening

RegenHub, LCA is a Colorado public benefit limited cooperative association organizing in Boulder that is two things at once: a member-owned entity governed by draft bylaws, and the host of the Boulder Ethereum Community Hub, which runs co-working, sessions, events, and an educational surface under the Ethereum Everywhere grant. CIS §01 · Hub §01, §02 The draft bylaws and the grant agreement already assign duties that depend on records: notices with legally timed delivery windows, capital accounts that must conserve, meetings with quorum rules, and monthly hub reporting whose figures gate a grant tranche. bylaws §2.4, §5.1 via PRDs · Hub §07, §08 The book of record that would carry those duties does not yet exist on the constrained substrate: the hub surface is greenfield, and the cooperative surface, which the PRD describes as partly built, is implemented fresh from the first migration under the plan. CIS §01, §09 · plan §2.1, §2.4

D-01.1 · BWho is affected

The invited members of the alpha cohort, who will sign agreements and be governed by what the records say. The organizers, who carry admission, notice, allocation, and minutes duties whether or not software exists to help. The hub team and its EE partners, who run sessions, events, and education and owe a monthly account of them. Counsel, who must ratify the bylaws the records will cite. The accountant who prepares K-1s from the capital records, and the grantor whose second tranche is gated on KPI compliance the hub must be able to demonstrate. plan §05, §06, §07 · Hub §08

D-01.1 · CWhy this matters now

Three clocks are running. The bylaws are moving toward ratification, and encoding duties against a moving text invites citation drift, which is why the plan keeps a draft-citation register rather than waiting (D7). The grant arc began May 22, 2026 and is dated: the first monthly report is due at the end of June, the KPI compliance review lands in mid-August, and the second tranche, $10,000 of the $20,000 total, is due August 31, 2026, gated on KPI achievement. Hub §02, §08 And the first real member onboarding is itself a gated step: until the system exists, the cooperative cannot admit members onto its own record. plan §2.4, §2.5, §05 Waiting pauses none of these; it only means the duties get carried by hand, outside any conserving record. inferred

D-01.1 · DWhat good would look like

One authoritative state where every figure drills to the events that produced it. A member signs in and sees what governs them: their agreement, their class, in time their balance history. The hub team runs applications, sessions, events, the KPI dashboard, and a monthly report against production data, with the EE team able to verify the figures independently. Reports are computed rather than reported. And the build itself is legible: the portal's build panel shows members the live state of the system being made for them, the same property the records will have once the system carries them. plan §07, §3.4 · Hub §07

D-01.1 · EConstraints

  • GitHub Pages and Supabase as principal infrastructure; other platforms only as critically needed. The substrate constraint, set by Todd and treated by the plan as binding. plan lede
  • No time estimates. Sequencing is by dependency strata, not calendar periods. plan §01
  • Decision and ratification nodes are human-owned; no item in the D or R series may be closed by an agent. plan §05
  • No secrets and no member PII in the repository; synthetic seeds only, until the gated moment of first real onboarding. plan §2.4 · D3
  • Privacy is a property of the record, not the screen; with a public anon key, RLS completeness is a hard release gate, not a quality goal. CIS principle 06 · plan §2.3
  • Conservation, traceability, and audit live in the database, not the interface. CIS principles 04, 05, 09
  • Nou augments and never acts alone; the gateway forwards the invoking user's permissions structurally. CIS principle 01 · plan §2.2 · W1.7
  • No Subchapter K vocabulary on RegenHub surfaces, enforced as a CI rule. plan §2.5 · W0.16

One item often mistaken for a constraint is, so far, a preference: a public repository from first commit is the proposed default under D3, on the open-source pathway, but it remains a decision the organizers have not yet recorded. plan §05

D-01.1 · FWhat this is not

Coordination Games infrastructure, which is a separate system. Counsel work product: bylaws ratification and the Hub Membership Agreement review are indexed as gates, not planned. The Xero general-ledger mirror, ENS resolvability, and on-chain attestation, all deferred beyond alpha. The Make.com orchestration layer, demoted to a named augmentation candidate. And Practice, CIS Phase 06, which is cultural work that software cannot deliver and a status board must not claim. plan §01

D-01.4 · APurpose

Establish the cooperative's book of record on both of its faces: as an entity, carrying membership, agreements, capital, and governance; and as a host, carrying applications, sessions, events, KPIs, and the grant lifecycle. The first delivery is a private alpha that real invited members and the hub team operate against production data. plan §2.1, §2.4

D-01.4 · BScope

The alpha gate set: all of W0; W1.1 through W1.8; W2.1 through W2.6; with decisions D1, D2, D3, D4, D6, and D7 recorded. plan §07

D-01.4 · COut of scope

W2.7 through W2.9 and waves W3 onward, which proceed against the same ledger after the gate rather than inside this charter. And everything the problem brief names as not this work: Coordination Games, counsel work product, the Xero mirror, ENS, attestation, the Make.com layer, and Practice. plan §01, §07

D-01.4 · DSuccess criteria

By name, from the worksheet: a real alpha, not a demo; hosting duties computed, not reported; records that carry their own audit; members who can see what governs them; a build legible to its members; pathway discipline held.

D-01.4 · ESponsor

Reserved to organizers. The corpus records one act of sponsor-shaped authority: the substrate constraint Todd set, which the plan treats as binding on everything downstream. Whether that names the sponsor is for the organizers to say, not for this document to assume. plan lede framing inferred

D-01.4 · FAccountable lead

Reserved. The operating loop names Todd as the human reviewing state, resolving the D and R ledger's queue, and approving wherever verification names a human. Proposed default: Todd, to confirm or replace at signoff. plan §08

D-01.4 · GConstraints

The hard list in the problem brief, by reference: the substrate constraint; dependency strata, not calendar; human-owned decisions; no PII or secrets in the repository; RLS completeness as a release gate; conservation in the database; Nou never acting alone; the vocabulary rule.

D-01.4 · HAuthorized by

Unsigned. Authorization is the one thing this document cannot reconstruct: it is the human act the charter exists to record. Proposed for organizer signoff. Under the proposed D3 posture, the natural moment is at or before the first commit, so the repository's first entry and the project's authorization are the same gesture. proposal

What does private mean for this alpha?

Pages publishes the shell publicly on the current plan, so the privacy promise lives entirely in the data layer. Whether a public shell is acceptable, or a proxy augmentation is wanted, is an open posture decision. plan §2.3

Q-03
Where does the public application form live?

Blocks W2.2 directly, and the W1.6 admission flow inherits the pattern. The substrate constraint adds a third, native option the PRDs did not contain, named in the plan as an explicit extension. plan §05 · Hub §11 · CIS §17

resolved by D2 recorded · owner organizers
Q-04
Does the cooperative or a separate entity hold the lease?

Whether West or the LCA holds the third-floor lease shapes how facility obligations enter the ledger; blocks only that detail (W3.6), not the wave. CIS §17 · plan §05 · D10

resolved by D10, with counsel awareness · owner organizers
Q-05
Is user research warranted for an invite-only cohort the organizers already know?

The framework treats research as essential only where the team's beliefs could be wrong in ways that would change the build. This cohort may make duties, not users, the discovery question; either way the judgment should be recorded, not defaulted. Phase 02 guide, decision 05

resolved by a recorded decision to run or waive D-02.5, with reasons · owner organizers
Q-06
What counts as prototype evidence here?

D-02.4 expects load-bearing assumptions tested before construction makes wrongness costly. The plan's W0 steps, each with a testable success state, can double as that evidence if so declared; otherwise the gap is real.

resolved by the Discovery movement naming the evidence standard · owner organizers, with Nou drafting

Attestation scope and key custody

07 · Method, Provenance, Next Movements

What was read, and what was verified. This baseline was first drawn from the Implementation Plan v0.1 and the Build Roadmap v0.2; at v0.2 the two PRDs have been read in full and every relayed CIS § and Hub § citation checked against source. None was found contradicted. The check enriched several facts: the entity form (a Colorado public benefit limited cooperative association), the hub's name (Boulder Ethereum Community Hub), the grant's shape ($20,000 in two tranches, May 22 to December 31, 2026, the second gated on KPI achievement), the lease question's parties (West or the LCA), and the counsel named for the Hub Membership Agreement review (Pote Law Firm). One wording tension surfaced and is carried into Discovery: CIS §01 describes the CIS as partly built and migrating progressively, while the plan implements fresh from a first migration; what, if anything, migrates in is for the organizers to state. Two source layers remain relayed: the grant agreement (via the Hub PRD) and the draft bylaws (via both PRDs and the plan).

What is reconstruction. Inception is being written after Discovery- and Definition-grade documents already exist. That inversion is named rather than hidden: source tags mark where content comes from the corpus and where it is inferred. Interest and influence ratings in the stakeholder map are working judgments; the relationship column is deliberately empty. Nothing in this document closes a decision; every item in the D and R series remains with the organizers.

The framework source. The scaffolds for D-01.1 through D-01.4 are the verbatim structures published in the Phase 01 Inception Guide v0.2; D-01.5 follows the inferred scaffold, marked as such in the Framework Explorer. The combined treatment of CIS and Hub Operations as one definition track with two faces follows the plan's own posture: one substrate, shared agents, agreements, treasury, and pathway discipline. CIS §09 · Hub §09 · plan §2.1 That framing choice is itself replaceable.

Next movements, in order. 02 Discovery consolidation (issued alongside this revision): the requirements index, feasibility verdicts across five dimensions, the risk register with owners and review points, and the two evidence decisions framed for organizers. 03 Definition synthesis: the architecture document as one synthesis of plan §2.2, §2.3, and §3.1; the ADR set recording what organizers actually decide on D1 through D10, one record per closed decision; the threat model the public-anon-key perimeter deserves; the build/buy/integrate canvas and stack record formalized from the stack delta; the definition of done lifted from §3.3. Each lands as its own movement in this same register.
