## Question

Whether a publicly visible app shell is acceptable for the alpha.

## Context and options

Proposed default: accept the public static shell; privacy enforced at the data layer; noindex; invite-only auth. Alternative augmentation: a proxy with access control in front of Pages.

## Proposed default

Stated in the context above; a proposal only. The decision is reserved to the named owner.

## Record of decision

Decided 2026-06-12 by Todd Youngblood (Ventures & Operations Steward).

The CIS adopts an open data stance: authentication via Supabase auth for private member functions, with a set of public pages that introduce the system and surface insensitive live data — metrics, KPIs, and aggregate measures — without requiring login. Privacy is enforced at the data layer through RLS: tables holding member PII, capital accounts, and patronage detail require authentication and role checks; public-facing tables (event attendance, KPI aggregates, hub metrics) are readable via the anon key. This posture serves accountability and transparency as cooperative principles, not just access control as a technical constraint. No proxy in front of Pages. Internal document pages retain noindex,nofollow.
