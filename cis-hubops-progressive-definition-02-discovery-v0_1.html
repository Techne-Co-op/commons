01 · Frame: Consolidation, Not Origination

The plan states it plainly: the PRDs are the authoritative specification. plan §01 Recasting them wholesale into the framework's requirements form would produce a second copy of the truth that drifts the moment either PRD is revised. So D-02.1 is satisfied here by reference plus a thin index that names where each requirement class lives and where its acceptance criteria sit. That is a deliberate choice, and it is overridable: if a standalone FR and NFR document is wanted later, the index below is its table of contents.

What this movement leaves out, so the omission is visible: no new research is originated; the economic and legal verdicts in section 03 rest on the documents alone, with neither the accountant nor counsel consulted, and both verdicts should be re-rendered when they are; and two source layers remain relayed rather than read, the grant agreement (via the Hub PRD) and the draft bylaws (via both PRDs and the plan).

05 · Evidence Standard, Proposed · D-02.4

resolves Q-06 if adopted · owner: organizers, with Nou drafting

The framework expects load-bearing assumptions to be tested before construction makes wrongness costly, and it expects the record to show, per prototype: the assumption tested, the result, and the decision that followed. The plan's W0 steps already carry that shape. Each is a milestone file with a context (the assumption), a testable success state (the test), a named verification (the result), and a status flip in the same PR (the decision). The proposal: declare the verified W0 step set to be the prototype-evaluation record for this project. No separate prototype theater; the substrate proves itself on the way to existing.

Three assumptions are the load-bearing ones the standard must cover before the first module wave begins:

  • The RLS-only perimeter holds: anonymous and cross-role probes cannot read or write beyond policy, with the anon key public by design. Evidence at W0.6, probe suite green. plan §2.3 · W0.6
  • The migration pipeline catches schema mistakes before they reach the single production environment: shadow verification fails the PR, and restore is a tested capability. Evidence at W0.4 and W0.14. plan §2.4, §2.5
  • The static shell, direct Supabase access under RLS, and the Edge Function rail can together carry the member portal: auth, scoped reads, and one privileged write path demonstrated end to end. Evidence at W0.5, W0.9, W0.12. plan §2.3 · W0.12

If the organizers adopt this, D-02.4 is satisfied prospectively by the ledger itself, and the adoption should be recorded as a one-line decision note in the repository so the standard is citable. If they do not, the gap is real and a separate prototype plan is owed before W1.

06 · User Research Decision, Framed · D-02.5

resolves Q-05 if adopted · owner: organizers

The framework's own test: research is essential when the team's beliefs could be wrong in ways that would change what gets built, and theater when the team would proceed identically regardless of findings. Its position is that a small amount of well-designed research with five people beats a broad program with fifty. Phase 02 guide, decision 05 Here the members are known and invited, and the duties come from bylaws and a grant rather than from preference discovery, so broad research would likely be theater. Two beliefs could still be wrong in build-changing ways: that the interfaces pass the member test (“a curious new member, not a co-op MBA”), and that the public application form is legible to the strangers it is for, locals, visitors, and digital nomads. CIS principle 02, §14 · Hub §02

The proposal: waive broad discovery research, with these reasons recorded; name the G1 operational walkthrough with a real invited member as the research instrument for the first belief, since the gate already requires exactly that; and add a brief usability pass with one or two applicants from outside the organizer circle to W2.2's verification for the second. Either way, the decision and its reasons belong in the record rather than in the default.
